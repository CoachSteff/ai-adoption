## The 5-Phase Adoption Engine

### Phase 1: Readiness Assessment
**Goal:** Where are we?
- AI maturity evaluation (Levels 1-5)
- Organizational capability assessment
- Gap analysis

### Phase 2: Strategic Prioritization
**Goal:** What do we do first?
- Use case identification
- Impact/Effort or VAF prioritization
- Business KPI alignment

### Phase 3: Building the Roadmap
**Goal:** How do we get there?
- 7-workstream planning model
- Resource allocation
- Timeline and milestones

### Phase 4: Measuring True Value
**Goal:** Was it worth it?
- **ROI**: Return on Investment (financial)
- **ROE**: Return on Employee (people)
- **ROF**: Return on Future (innovation)

### Phase 5: Scaling to Production
**Goal:** The "AI Factory"
- ModelOps implementation
- Infrastructure scaling
- Sustained adoption

## Shadow AI: The Hidden Risk

**What is Shadow AI?**
Employees using AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) without official approval or oversight.

**The Stats:**
- 60-80% of employees use unauthorized AI tools
- Most organizations unaware of the extent
- Creates data security, compliance, and quality risks

**4-Step Management Plan:**
1. **Establish AI-Acceptable Use Policy** - Clear guidelines, not bans
2. **Train and Educate** - Build AI literacy, show approved options
3. **Monitor and Audit** - Track usage without being punitive
4. **Create Innovation Framework** - Easy process to request new tools

**Key Principle:** Approach with curiosity, not punishment. Shadow AI reveals unmet needs.

## EU Compliance Landscape

### EU AI Act
- **Timeline:** High-risk systems by Aug 2026, full compliance 2027
- **Risk Categories:** Minimal, Limited, High, Unacceptable
- **Penalties:** Up to €35M or 7% of global revenue
- **Action:** Map your AI systems to risk categories now

### NIS2 Directive
- **Status:** Already in effect (Oct 2024)
- **Scope:** ~160,000 EU entities (critical infrastructure, essential services)
- **Focus:** Cybersecurity, supply chain, incident reporting
- **Action:** Integrate AI security into NIS2 compliance program

### GDPR + AI
- **Challenge:** AI amplifies privacy risks (training data, decision transparency)
- **Requirements:** Data minimization, purpose limitation, explainability
- **Action:** Conduct AI-specific DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment)

## The Human-Centric Operating Model

### 1. Employee Value Proposition (EVP)
**Reframe AI from threat to opportunity:**
- "AI as Co-Pilot" not replacement
- Emphasize augmentation, not automation
- Show career growth pathways in AI era

### 2. Change Management
**Apply McKinsey "Rewired" framework:**
- Leadership commitment and role-modeling
- Compelling change story
- Skills and capabilities development
- Formal mechanisms (policies, incentives)

### 3. Upskilling Framework (6 Steps)
1. Assess current AI literacy across workforce
2. Define role-specific AI competencies
3. Design tiered learning paths (awareness → proficiency → expertise)
4. Deliver mixed-format training (workshops, self-paced, hands-on)
5. Embed learning in workflow
6. Measure and iterate

### 4. Shadow AI Management
(See 4-Step Plan above)

## Prioritization Frameworks

### For Low Maturity (Level 1-2)
**Impact/Effort Matrix**
- High Impact, Low Effort = Do first
- High Impact, High Effort = Plan strategically
- Low Impact, Low Effort = Quick wins
- Low Impact, High Effort = Avoid

### For Medium Maturity (Level 3)
**Value/Actionability/Feasibility (VAF)**
- **Value:** Strategic importance + ROI potential
- **Actionability:** Data availability + organizational readiness
- **Feasibility:** Technical complexity + resource requirements

### For High Maturity (Level 4-5)
**Advanced Frameworks:**
- Differentiability Index (competitive advantage potential)
- Task Automatability Index (technical feasibility + human judgment need)
- Strategic Portfolio Balancing (horizon 1/2/3 mix)
